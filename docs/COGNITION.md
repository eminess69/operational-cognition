# Operational Cognition

Operational cognition means cognition treated as operational infrastructure.

In this repository, the term describes a disciplined public framework for investigating long-horizon AI engineering behavior through continuity, replayability, lineage, bounded claims, and failure reconstruction. It is concerned with how AI-assisted engineering systems preserve or lose operational coherence across tools, sessions, environments, repositories, and evidence trails.

Operational cognition emphasizes:

- continuity
- replayability
- lineage
- bounded claims
- public/private boundaries
- evidence discipline
- long-horizon coherence
- failure reconstruction

It does not assert:

- AGI
- consciousness
- that the model black box has been solved
- substrate performance beyond what public evidence supports
- access to private Belief Ledger or Inside Voice implementation details

## Doctrine

Operational Cognition treats engineering reasoning artifacts as infrastructure that must be inspectable at the public boundary. A proof pack should show what was investigated, which evidence was used, how claims were bounded, what remains unresolved, and how another investigator could replay the public trail.

The public artifacts are not raw cognition dumps. They are sanitized summaries designed to preserve evidence lineage without revealing private substrate state, hidden chain-of-thought, private scoring, harmonic traceback details, or implementation internals.

## Operational interpretability

Operational interpretability is behavioral and system-level interpretability of long-horizon AI systems.

It asks whether an investigation can reconstruct how an AI-assisted engineering process moved from inputs to actions, failures, mitigations, and claims using public evidence and lineage summaries. This is distinct from mechanistic interpretability of model weights. It does not attempt to explain neuron-level, activation-level, or parameter-level model internals.

Operational interpretability focuses on:

- what the system was asked to do
- what public evidence was available
- what actions or recommendations were produced
- where continuity degraded
- where replay diverged
- which claims are observed, inferred, hypothetical, or unresolved
- which private details are intentionally outside the public boundary

## Claim Boundary

Operational Cognition uses evidence-first language. Claims should be marked as observed, inferred, hypothesis, or unresolved. Contradictions should remain visible until resolved by evidence. Ambiguity should be preserved rather than flattened into false certainty.

No proof pack should claim AGI, consciousness, superintelligence, or a solved black box. The standard is public engineering discipline, not speculative cognition marketing.
