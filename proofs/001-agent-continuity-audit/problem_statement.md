# Problem Statement

Long-horizon agent systems can fail in ways that ordinary issue-level debugging does not fully reconstruct.

The planned investigation examines failures caused by:

- continuity degradation
- environmental drift
- tool-state mismatch
- architecture forgetting
- replay gaps
- weak provenance

## Investigation Question

Can long-horizon agent failures be reconstructed through replayable public evidence, continuity drift analysis, and operational lineage summaries better than ordinary issue-level debugging?

## Scope

The first proof targets public investigation of OpenClaw and OpenHands. The proof will focus on environmental and engineering continuity failure modes, not private model internals or speculative cognition claims.

## Current Status

This document defines the problem only. Evidence has not yet been collected or analyzed.
