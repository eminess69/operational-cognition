# Continuity Survivability Taxonomy

Each category below is an initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation. These are possible compression outcomes, not conclusions about either target system.

## Preserved continuity

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Continuity is preserved when the compressed representation, retained raw ranges, source refs, and surrounding metadata allow an auditor to reconstruct the relevant continuity dimension without material loss for the audit question.

## Partially reconstructable continuity

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Continuity is partially reconstructable when coarse sequence, major decisions, or final state survive, but fine-grained tool results, omitted branches, timestamps, or source authority require inference.

## Irreversible condensation

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Continuity undergoes irreversible condensation when original detail is replaced by summary text without enough retained IDs, hashes, snapshots, or source references to reconstruct the omitted range.

## Contradiction flattening

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Contradiction flattening occurs when conflicting evidence, corrections, rejected alternatives, or uncertainty are represented as a single resolved summary statement.

## Replay survivability loss

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Replay survivability loss occurs when visible playback remains possible but the compressed artifact no longer carries enough state to replay why the path occurred.

## Causal lineage collapse

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Causal lineage collapse occurs when the links between context, tool results, memory, environment state, and later action are omitted or merged beyond operational reconstruction.

## Intent survivability drift

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Intent survivability drift occurs when the compressed context preserves a task label or goal but loses constraints, authority, timing, scope changes, or prohibitions that shaped valid action.

## Recovery path loss

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Recovery path loss occurs when retries, fallback behavior, context-overflow handling, aborts, restarts, or cleanup steps are summarized without enough event lineage to reconstruct the recovery loop.

## Temporal ambiguity introduction

Initial operational taxonomy subject to evidence validation.

Temporal ambiguity is introduced when compression removes or weakens event ordering, timestamps, before/after boundaries, or compaction insertion points needed to reason about causality.
